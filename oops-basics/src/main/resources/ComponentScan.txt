
@ComponentScan(basePackages = "in.novopay")
This means Spring will scan all subpackages starting with in.novopay, regardless of whether they belong to the microservice codebase or a separate library JAR.
When you build/run your app, the classes from infra-cache JAR are added to your application’s classpath.
From the JVM’s perspective, there’s no difference between classes in your microservice and classes in that lib — they’re all just under in.novopay... packages on the classpath.


@EnableJpaRepositories(basePackages = {"in.novopay.*"})
@EntityScan(basePackages = {"in.novopay.*"})
Any JPA entities and repositories under in.novopay... (whether in the service or libs) will be registered.


Your microservice and infra libs share the same base package namespace (in.novopay...).
Because of this, when you tell Spring to scan in.novopay, it ends up scanning both your service code and all infra libraries — since they all fall under the same root package.

If the infra modules were under a totally different package name (say com.external.infra.cache), then you’d have to explicitly add that package to @ComponentScan / @EntityScan / @EnableJpaRepositories.
